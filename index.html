Species

Red Snapper

The red snapper is a popular game fish found in the Gulf of Mexico and along the southern Atlantic coast of the United States. It is prized for its delicious, white meat and is a favorite of anglers of all skill levels.

Grouper

Grouper are large, bottom-dwelling fish that are popular with both commercial and recreational anglers. They are found in warm waters around the world and are prized for their flaky, white meat.

Mahi Mahi

Mahi mahi, also known as dorado, is a popular game fish found in warm waters around the world. It is prized for its bright colors and acrobatic fights, as well as its delicious, mild-tasting meat.

    • Fishing Tips

    Tip #1

    When casting, it's important to keep your movements smooth and fluid to avoid spooking the fish. Begin by holding your rod at a comfortable angle, with the reel facing downward. Keep your elbow close to your body and use your wrist to control the motion of the rod. As you begin to cast, use a gentle forward motion, smoothly accelerating the rod to generate enough speed to propel your bait or lure to your desired location. Once your bait or lure lands in the water, keep your rod tip up to maintain control over your line and to detect any bites. Additionally, be sure to vary your casting distance and direction to increase your chances of finding fish. By keeping your movements smooth and fluid, and varying your casting distance and direction, you'll increase your chances of success when casting.

    Tip #2

    When trolling, it's important to use the right lure or bait to attract the fish you're targeting. Different species of fish prefer different types of lures or bait, so do your research beforehand to determine what works best for the fish in your area. Additionally, pay attention to the speed at which you're trolling. The speed at which you troll can make a big difference in the success of your fishing. As a general rule of thumb, a speed of around 2-3 miles per hour is a good starting point, but you may need to adjust your speed depending on the species you're targeting and the conditions on the water. Finally, pay attention to your surroundings and try to mimic the natural movements of the baitfish in the area. By using the right lure or bait, adjusting your speed, and mimicking the movements of baitfish, you'll increase your chances of success when trolling.

    Tip #3

    When jigging, it's important to vary your jigging motion to entice fish to bite. Try a combination of short, sharp jerks followed by longer, slower lifts. Experiment with the rhythm and speed of your jigging to see what works best for the fish in your area. Additionally, pay attention to the depth at which the fish are biting and adjust the depth of your jig accordingly. By varying your technique and paying attention to the fish's behavior, you'll increase your chances of success when jigging.
